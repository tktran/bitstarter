Takeoff is a modern course planner for computer science students at CSULB. It's a fast, intuitive HTML5 app to translate your past and present CS courses into the real world skills employers look for. In 10 minutes, you'll set a personal plan to get the programming and development jobs you want after graduation. You'll save time nagivating the CS curriculum and have a detailed roadmap taking off with your career after graduation.

You currently plan your classes using a fragmented combination of MyCSULB, the online CSULB schedule, and help from your advisor. Aside from class registration, Takeoff combines these services: class availability info, personalized course roadmaps, and academic and career guidance. You need to do three things: start a free account, enter your course data, and your academic/career plans. Takeoff will generate a plan for you to use and update throughout your college career. Test

FAQ

Do I have to pay for anything?
No. I might cover the costs of running Takeoff using unobtrusive ads on the side, but nothing otherwise. Takeoff doesn't have a clear business model; it's meant as a service to fellow CS students.
Who's behind Takeoff?
I'm Tan Tran, a junior at CSULB majoring in CS/Math. I design and program Takeoff in my free time as a personal programming project with guidance from the Coursera course on Startup Engineering. Takeoff runs on HTML5, Javascript, and some nifty machine learning algorithms for recommendations.
